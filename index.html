Hello World.

Born in Varese, Italy, I pursued my interests in Philosophy at the University of Milan, earning both a bachelor's and a master's degree. There, I discovered and fostered my interests in Philosophy of Science and Historical & Social Epistemology, graduating in 2023 with a thesis discussing convergences between Mach and Pragmatist epistemology, and in 2025 with a thesis on the epistemology of Big Scientific Collaborations, with particular attention to the Event Horizon Telescope research collective. My research interests span traditional topics in the Integrated History & Philosophy of science to Historical and Social Epistemology, with particular attention to the conditions that allow and affect the production of collective knowledge. I'm actually working on a project focusing on the role of Trust in Big Scientific Collaborations, using multimessenger case studies.

Beyond academic research, I am deeply engaged in science communication and public engagement. I am an active member of the G.V. Schiaparelli Astronomical Observatory, where I coordinate volunteer activities, organize cultural events, and develop educational programs. I have experience in team coordination, event organization, and effective communication across diverse audiences.
